Case Study: "The Data Disconnect at BharatTech Solutions"

 

Characters:

 

●     Anjali Sharma: CEO of BharatTech Solutions

●     Rajesh Kumar: Head of Data & Analytics (the student's role in this scenario)

●     Deepak Singhania: Head of Marketing

●     Priya Patel: Head of Sales

●     Sameer Khan: Head of Human Resources

●     Rina Gupta: Head of Product Development

 

Setting the Scene:

 

BharatTech Solutions, a mid-sized and rapidly growing tech company based in Bengaluru, India, offers innovative SaaS products to businesses across various sectors. Despite its success, CEO Anjali Sharma felt a growing frustration. Every Monday morning, her executive team meetings were a series of fragmented reports. Deepak would present marketing campaign performance, Priya would detail sales numbers, Sameer would share HR stats, and Rina would update on product milestones.

"It's like looking at different pieces of a jigsaw puzzle, but never seeing the whole picture," Anjali often remarked. "How do I know if our marketing spend in Delhi is actually driving sales in Mumbai? Or if our high employee turnover in Chennai is impacting product development timelines? I need to make quick, strategic decisions, but I'm spending half my week just trying to connect the dots across departments."

Anjali knew BharatTech was data-rich, but insight-poor. She needed a unified view, a single source of truth that could provide immediate, actionable insights to guide the company's growth trajectory. She called Rajesh Kumar, her newly appointed Head of Data & Analytics, into her office.

 

The Challenge Handed to Rajesh (Business Understanding):

 

"Rajesh," Anjali began, "your top priority is to solve this. I need a Company-Wide Performance Dashboard. This isn't just about pretty charts; it's about connecting our operations to our financial outcomes. I want to see how each department contributes to our overall health, understand our customer journey from awareness to retention, and identify potential bottlenecks before they impact our bottom line. I need to know 'what happened,' 'why it happened,' and ideally, 'what we should do about it.' Can you deliver this?"

Rajesh accepted the challenge, understanding that Anjali wasn't asking for just data; she was asking for business value through interconnected insights. This was the critical Business Understanding phase defining the problem and the desired outcome.

 

The Data Detective Work (Data Understanding & Preparation):

 

Rajesh's first task was to become a data detective. He met with each department head to understand their data sources, systems, and key metrics.

●     Deepak (Marketing): His team used a mix of Google Ads, Facebook Ads Manager, and an internal campaign tracking system. Key metrics included:

○     Cost Per Acquisition (CPA): How much does it cost us to acquire one new customer?

■     Formula: Total Marketing Spend / Number of New Customers Acquired

○     Click-Through Rate (CTR): How effective are our ads at grabbing attention?

■     Formula: (Number of Clicks / Number of Impressions) * 100%

○     Conversion Rate: What percentage of people who visit our landing page actually sign up for a demo or product?

■     Formula: (Number of Conversions / Number of Website Visitors) * 100%

●     Priya (Sales): Her team used a Salesforce CRM system and had quarterly revenue reports in Excel. Key metrics included:

○     Total Revenue: What is our top-line sales figure?

■     Formula: Sum of all sales transactions

○     Sales Growth (YoY): How much have our sales grown compared to the same period last year?

■     Formula: ((Current Period Revenue - Prior Period Revenue) / Prior Period Revenue) * 100%

○     Customer Lifetime Value (CLV): How much revenue can we expect from an average customer over their entire relationship with us?

■     Formula: (Average Purchase Value * Average Purchase Frequency) * Average Customer Lifespan

●     Sameer (HR): His department used a human resource information system (HRIS) for employee data and a separate system for annual surveys. Key metrics included:

○     Employee Turnover Rate: What percentage of our workforce is leaving each quarter?

■     Formula: (Number of Separations / Average Number of Employees) * 100%

○     Employee Satisfaction Score (ESS): What is the average score from our latest employee survey?

■     Formula: Sum of all satisfaction scores / Total number of employees surveyed

○     Time to Hire: How long does it take us to fill an open position?

■     Formula: Date of Offer Acceptance - Date of Job Opening

●     Rina (Product Development): Her team tracked project timelines and bug reports in Jira. Key metrics were mostly project-based, like "features delivered on time" and "bug resolution time," which Rajesh noted would be harder to integrate directly but important for context.

Rajesh realized the data was in different formats, systems, and levels of granularity. This was the massive Data Preparation challenge. He decided to use Zoho Analytics for its robust data pipeline capabilities to connect to Salesforce, Google Ads, Facebook Ads, and import Excel/CSV files from HR and internal systems. He used Zoho's data transformation features to clean, merge, and structure the data into a unified model. For some Google-specific data sources, he planned to use Google Data Studio (Looker Studio) for direct connections if Zoho's integration proved complex.

 

Building the Insights (Modeling/Analysis):

 

With the data pipeline established and clean data flowing, Rajesh began building the interactive dashboards. He started with descriptive analytics, visualizing current and historical trends for each key metric.

●     He created a "Marketing Performance" tab showing CPA, CTR, and Conversion Rates for different campaigns and regions.

●     A "Sales Overview" tab displayed Total Revenue, Sales Growth, and CLV broken down by product and customer segment.

●     An "HR Health" tab showed Turnover Rates, ESS, and Time to Hire.

The real breakthrough came when he started cross-referencing. He noticed a dip in sales conversion rates in certain regions that corresponded with a recent reduction in marketing spend (connecting Marketing to Sales). He also observed that higher employee turnover in the Mumbai office seemed to correlate with slower product feature delivery (connecting HR to Product). These were preliminary diagnostic insights understanding why something happened by finding correlations.

 

Refinement and Acceptance (Evaluation):

 

Rajesh presented the initial draft to Anjali and the department heads. There were many rounds of feedback. Anjali wanted to see the impact of specific marketing campaigns on CLV. Deepak wanted filters for different advertising channels. Sameer asked for a drill-down by employee tenure.

This iterative process of getting feedback, making adjustments, and ensuring the dashboards answered the right questions was the Evaluation phase. Rajesh refined the dashboards in Zoho Analytics and Google Data Studio, ensuring they were not just visually appealing but also accurate, interactive, and, most importantly, actionable.

 

Empowering Decision-Making (Deployment & Business Value):

 

Finally, the Company-Wide Performance Dashboard was deployed. It was a secure, web-based dashboard accessible to Anjali and her leadership team.

●     Before: Fragmented data, slow decision-making, missed opportunities.

●     After: Anjali could now log in every morning and see a real-time, consolidated view of BharatTech's performance. She could identify:

○     Which marketing campaigns were most efficient (low CPA with high Conversion Rate).

○     Whether the increase in marketing spend was truly driving Sales Growth.

○     Regions with high Employee Turnover that might need immediate HR intervention to prevent negative impacts on Product Development timelines.

This unified dashboard transformed executive meetings from report-sharing sessions into strategic discussions. Anjali could now ask: "Our Mumbai Employee Turnover Rate is 15% this quarter, significantly higher than our target of 8%. This seems to coincide with a slowdown in product updates from that team. What specific actions can HR take this week to address this, and how will we measure the impact on product delivery in the next quarter?"

BharatTech Solutions had moved from being data-rich and insight-poor to being truly data-driven. The analytics lifecycle, carefully followed by Rajesh, had delivered immense business value by enabling faster, more informed, and proactive decision-making across the entire organization.
